IB Higher Level Physics

Course Description

IB Higher Level Physics is an extension of the standard level course. Students that have successfully completed the SL curriculum and strive to take a deeper look at the core concepts while being introduced to modern physics theories are encouraged to enroll. Because students already have a foundation from the previous year of study, HL physics covers less material but takes a more comprehensive look at the concepts; including higher expectations in regard to problem-solving, analysis, and communication skills. The format of the class is less formal, encouraging students to actively engage in material through group projects, full class discussions, and independent research. Internal Assessment portfolios are carried over from the previous year and an additional 20 hours of practical laboratory work is the foundation for the course. The course begins with a look at the history behind modern physics, working backward to review the previous year's material, while the second semester focuses on critical analysis and exam preparation skills.
