IB Visual Arts

Course Description

2D Art

This course affords students the opportunity to work in a variety of mediums in the application of their ideas through drawing, painting, printmaking, illustration, and design. Individual programs are developed with the student's cultural background, personal needs and interest, as well as the influence of the Japanese culture playing an important part in program direction and theme. The aims of the program are to provide students with opportunities to develop their creative selves, train their visual awareness of various cultures, find their own best way of expression, develop skills, and encourage a lively, inquiring, and informed attitude toward art and design in all its forms, both in history and today.

IB Ceramics & Photography

IB Photography and Ceramics final exam consists of an art exhibit and an interview with an I.B. art examiner. Students work independently on selected themes and are guided by the teacher when needed. Critiques are an integral part of the program and help the student develop a working vocabulary to express the ideas behind the work that they have completed at the end of the two-year cycle. I.B. Art is offered at Standard and Higher levels. All students must also keep a working journal. Quality is emphasized over quantity.

Architecture

By participating in field studies, research, and studio work, students in this class develop an understanding of architecture as a profession and learn to articulate their own design ideas. During this two-year program, they gain an understanding of the history of architecture and of the influences and contributions of various architects from around the world. While participating in an individualized study of architecture as a creative art form, students develop their visual and spatial awareness, apply design principles, and refine sketching techniques. Each student develops and expresses his own talents by producing drawings and constructing models of original commercial and residential designs.
