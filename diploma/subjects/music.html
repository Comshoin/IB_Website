IB Music

Course Description

11th Grade

This two-year course prepares the student for I.B. Music-Standard or Higher Level examination. Students learn basic music theory, composition, musical form and analysis, as they study music throughout history. Prerequisite: Creative Music or consent of the instructor

12th Grade

This is the second of a two-year course which prepares the student for I.B. Music-Standard or Higher Level examination. Students learn basic music theory, composition, musical form and analysis, as they study music throughout history. Prerequisite: IB Music in Grade 11

Units (11th and 12th Grade)

  • Unit 1: Basic theory, Introduction to Music Perception and Listening
  • Unit 2: Music for Worship
  • Unit 3: Variations on a Theme
  • Unit 4: Program Music
  • Unit 5: Contrapuntal Music
  • Unit 6: Sonata-Allegro
  • Unit 7: The Stage
  • Unit 8: Post-Tonalism
  • Unit 9: Popular Music

Prescribed Works

  • Classical Symphony (Prokofiev)
  • Yellow River Piano Concerto (Xian)

Projects

IB Music Investigation (as part of the external assessment)

Music Listening and Analysis

During this time, students learn about the general practices in the music with reference to the units, listen and analyze some examples, and write observations in notebooks.

Composition

Compositions are based on a general practice of the music that was just studied. Some compositions may be brief, just to help the students understand the concept while others will be longer.

Performance Ensembles

Students taking IB Music should be enrolled either in band or choir.

Assessment

Assessment is based first on classroom participation, composition projects, successful completion of assignments, and overall performance in the classroom. Excessive absences (more than 3 in a quarter or 5 in a semester) will result in a lower grade.

Homework

Much work will be completed in class. The classroom is available daily from 8:00-4:15 to work on assignments should more time be needed. All assignments are expected to be completed by the due date. Incomplete homework will result in poor performance in class.

Resources

  • Pencils, folder, classroom computers
  • Reference Text: Concise History of Western Music by Barbary Russano Hanning
  • Anthology: Norton Anthology of Western Music
  • Outline and study guide
  • Software programs: Finale 2011; Garageband

Other Information

All students are respectful of each other.

  • As an audience, students listen to each other's compositions.
  • Offer constructive criticism and positive feedback.
  • Participate positively in class discussions.

Absence does not excuse an assignment nor extend a due date.

Due Dates

  • March 14, 2014 - Music Investigation
  • March 21, 2014 - Internal Assessment (Performance and/or composition)

Checkpoints

October 22; January 10; February 28; March 21 (1/4 performance and all documentation). At each checkpoint, students in HL MUST have one 3-6 minute composition completed and 1/4 of their 20 minute performance recorded.
