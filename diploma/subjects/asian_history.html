IB Higher Level Asian History

Course Description

This is a one-year course taken by students in the Senior year after they have completed IB Standard Level Contemporary World History in the Junior year. IB East Asian Studies follows the prescribed course criteria as determined by the IB program. Within these guidelines, the course will cover the major geographical, cultural, socio-economic, historical and intellectual trends and events that have impacted Japan and China in the period from approximately 1800 to 1975. Students, along with the teacher, will decide which aspects and movements will be studied in depth in preparation for the IB examination. The focus will be on the political, military, economic and social history of both Japan and China. The course is designed to help students understand the present by caring about what happened in the past.

Assessment

A variety of assignments including essays, research projects, detailed map work, and simulations will be used, in addition to quizzes, tests and exams in determining grades. The ultimate assessment, of course, will be the IB Internal Assessment and the IB Exam. The criteria regarding success for the IA and Final Exam are prescribed by the IB and will be given as handouts during relevant times in the year.

Homework Expectations

Students are expected to keep up with the reading assignments. Students can expect 45 minutes to one hour of homework each day mostly in the form of reading and response. Ample time will be given for research assignments that will be over and above the reading requirements.

Resources

The required reading for this course comes from two major textbooks

  • Japan: Tradition and Transformation by Craig and Reischauer, Houghton Mifflin.
  • The Rise of Modern China by Immanuel Hsu, Oxford Press.

In addition to the above, students will be given numerous handouts that they should organize into a special folder. They should also have a notebook where they will keep notes from videos, class discussion and the readings.

Other Information

Students must achieve a grade of 4 or higher in the IB Standard Level Contemporary World History course in order to be admitted into the IB Higher Level Asian History course.
