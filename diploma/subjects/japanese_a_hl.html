IB Japanese A HL

Course Description

This two-year course:

  1. introduces students to a range of texts from different periods, styles and genres
  2. develops in students the ability to engage in close, detailed analysis of individual texts and make relevant connections
  3. develops the students' powers of expression, both in oral and written communication
  4. encourages students to recognize the importance of the contexts in which texts are written and received
  5. encourages, through the study of texts, an appreciation of the different perspectives of people from other cultures, and how these perspectives construct meaning
  6. encourages students to appreciate the formal, stylistic and aesthetic qualities of texts
  7. promotes in students an enjoyment of, and lifelong interest in, language and literature.
  8. develops in students an understanding of how language, culture and context determine the ways in which meaning is constructed in texts
  9. encourages students to think critically about the different interactions between text, audience and purpose.

Outline

Part 1: Language in Cultural Context

Texts are chosen from a variety of sources, genres and media.

Part 2: Language and Mass Communication

Texts are chosen from a variety of sources, genres and media.

Part 3: Literature - Texts and Contexts

HL: Three texts, one of which is a text in translation chosen from the prescribed literature in translation (PLT) list and one from the prescribed list of authors (PLA) for the language A studied. The other may be chosen freely.

Part 4: Literature - Critical Study

HL: Three texts, all of which are chosen from the prescribed list of authors (PLA) for the language A studied.
